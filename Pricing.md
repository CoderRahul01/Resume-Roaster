# Resume Roaster — Pricing Plan
### What's Free, What's Paid, and Why It's Designed This Way

---

## The Philosophy

Free must feel genuinely useful — not crippled. Paid must feel obviously worth it — not forced.

The roast is free because it creates an emotional reaction. A 3/10 score stings. That sting is what converts. The rewrite is paid because it resolves the pain the roast just created. That's the loop.

---

## Tier Structure

### Free Plan — "Get Roasted"

**Price:** ₹0 — no login, no credit card, nothing

**What they get:**

- Full AI roast of their resume (6 critique points, scored out of 10)
- Each critique point includes: an emoji, a sharp title, and a specific 2–3 sentence explanation
- An overall score from 1–10 with a one-line verdict (e.g., "This resume wouldn't survive the first ATS filter")
- Rate limit: 5 roasts per IP per hour — enough for genuine use, not enough for abuse

**What they do NOT get:**

- The rewritten resume
- ATS keyword suggestions
- Section-by-section improvement notes
- Cover letter
- LinkedIn optimization
- Any ability to download or export

**The purpose of the free tier:** It's not charity. It's a sales funnel. A good roast should make the user feel slightly embarrassed, clearly aware of the problem, and uncertain whether they can fix it alone. That's when the paywall lands.

---

### Paid Plan — "Get Fixed" — ₹499 One-Time

**Price:** ₹499 (one-time, per resume, no subscription)

**What they get:**

- Full AI rewrite of their resume content (preserving original section structure and layout order)
- Improvements applied across: Summary, Work Experience bullets, Skills, Projects, Certifications
- ATS-optimized language: strong action verbs, quantified achievements, keyword density aligned with their target industry
- Content only — format is preserved (no reformatting, no template changes)
- Delivered instantly on the `/success` page after payment
- One-time use per payment (signature verified, cannot be replayed)

**What they do NOT get (yet — these are upsell opportunities later):**

- LinkedIn profile rewrite
- Cover letter
- Multiple resume versions for different job roles
- Tracked changes view (showing exactly what changed)

---

### Future Paid Add-Ons (Month 3+, Build Only After Validating Core)

These should not be built before launch. Add them only after seeing real user behavior.

**Cover Letter — ₹199 add-on**
Shown on the `/success` page after rewrite delivery. One click, one additional payment, one Claude call. The user pastes the job description URL or text, Claude writes a matching cover letter. 40% of paying users will add this because the momentum is already there.

**LinkedIn Optimizer — ₹299 add-on**
Also shown on `/success`. Rewrites the LinkedIn summary, headline, and top 3 experience bullets for the same role. Different from resume format — shorter, more conversational, algorithmically keyword-heavy. Separate Claude call with a different system prompt.

**Re-Roast (After Edits) — ₹99**
User has edited their resume after the rewrite and wants to confirm it's now better. Cheap re-roast. Creates a "before vs after" score comparison. This is also shareable — "I went from 3/10 to 8/10 with Resume Roaster" is a social post.

**Placement Cell / Bulk Plan — ₹5,000/month**
Unlocks a simple dashboard where a placement coordinator submits up to 200 resume texts per month and gets back roast reports as a CSV. No fancy UI needed to start — validate with manual email delivery first, then build the dashboard.

## Pricing Psychology — Why These Numbers

**₹499 is the sweet spot for India's IT market for three reasons:**

One — it's below the mental "is this a subscription trap?" threshold. ₹499 one-time feels like buying a book, not subscribing to a service. No anxiety about forgetting to cancel.

Two — it's cheap enough that a job seeker earning ₹4–25 LPA doesn't hesitate. If getting a better job is worth anything, it's worth ₹499. The opportunity cost of NOT fixing their resume (missing a job offer) is infinitely higher.

Three — it's high enough to signal quality. Free tools are everywhere. ₹499 says "this is a real product with real output." A ₹49 price would actually convert worse because it feels low-effort.

**The ₹199 and ₹299 add-ons** are priced below the psychological "do I need to think about this?" threshold. After spending ₹499, ₹199 more feels trivial. This is the upsell principle — the first payment is the hard one.

## What the Paywall Should Say (Copy Guidance)

The paywall banner copy matters more than the paywall design. Here's what it should communicate:

**Headline:** "Your resume scored [X]/10. Here's the fixed version."

**Sub-copy:** "Our AI rewrote your resume with ATS-optimized language, stronger action verbs, and quantified achievements. One-time ₹499 — no subscription, no account needed."

**CTA button:** "Get My Fixed Resume — ₹499"

**Below the button (trust signals):**
- "Instant delivery. No waiting."
- "Razorpay secured payment."
- "Your resume text is never stored on our servers."

**What NOT to say:** Don't say "Premium Plan." Don't say "Upgrade." Don't say "Subscribe." All of these add friction. Say "Get My Fixed Resume" — the action, not the tier name.
